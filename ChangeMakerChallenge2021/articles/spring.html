An Environmentally-Friendly Spring Season

As the chilling temperatures of winter breeze away, warmer weather begins to settle in, and feelings of rebirth and renewal bloom, there is no better time to commit to going green than this upcoming spring season. It is during this season of renewal and fresh beginnings that we begin to feel the urge to spend more time outdoors and enjoy the beauty of nature. Let’s do our absolute best to conserve that beauty and help out our community simultaneously.

Here are a few tips to reduce your environmental footprint throughout this spring season:

1. Declutter your belongings

Be honest -- what percentage of your belongings do you actually need that aren’t just sitting in a closet for that one day where you mightttt happen to need it that never seems to come?

Chances are: there's quite a bit of stuff you don’t actually need. Take the initiative. You’ll feel much better afterwards.

Don’t know what to do with it?

Here are a few options:

  • Donations

    • Chances are there’s probably a friend or family member that’s looking for the exact thing you’re trying to get rid of - given it’s in decent condition of course. Ask around! And if you can’t find somebody willing to take it, just drop it off at one at one of Florida’s many donate centers. Not only would you be minimizing waste (and therefore your environmental footprint), but you would also be supporting a larger cause for those who need it most! Below are some of the links with their information:

      • https://goodwillsouthflorida.org/donation-centers

        • For any questions about what you can and cannot donate, click here

      • https://satruck.org/

      • https://www.browardoutreachcenter.org/springcleaning-miami-broward.php

  • Can’t Donate it? Recycle it!

    • According to a U.S. EPA report, recycling only 1 ton of paper saves 3.3 cubic yards of landfill space, as well as enough energy to power the average American home for six months. Consider recycling old newspapers, old books, all those school worksheets… And if you have any doubts about what you can or can’t recycle, check out CleansSteps’ Recycling Guidelines!

2. Declutter your belongings

Many products used in conventional products biodegrade into more toxic, persistent, and bioaccumulative chemicals, which threatens aquatic life. Vinegar, water and baking soda are great alternatives! Use natural fiber sponges and cleaning agents that are biodegradable, phosphate-free, chlorine-free, and unscented. Check out this website for further information on which products to use!

3. Stop Using Paper Towels

Producing paper towels requires things like trees, chemicals, fuel, water, and electricity—contributing to millions of metric tons of CO2 annually. Switch to reusable towels instead. Buy a few cheap ones or even cut up a few old t-shirts that are lying around. It’s a simple and great way to reduce waste.

4. Be energy efficient

Want to save hundreds of dollars a year AND reduce your environmental footprint? Switch out your incandescent bulbs to LED ones.

They use less energy, last longer, and save money. An incandescent bulb has an average lifespan of 1,200 hours, while an LED bulb will last you for 20,000 hours. And according to Lighting Info 2019, you’re able to save up to $600 a year just by switching all the lights in your home.

5. Check to see whether products you consistently use obtain ingredients that harm you or the environment. For example:

  • Exfoliators and facial cleansers with microbeads/microplastics, since these beads are polluting our waterways and making their ways into our bodies.

    • Watch out for ingredients that show microbeads, micro abrasives, polypropylene, or polyethylene

  • Shampoos and soaps with sulfates

    • Sodium Laureth Sulfate (SLS) is a common chemical compound in cosmetics that is often contaminated with probable human carcinogens.

    • The chemical isn't readily biodegradable, which means it builds up in the environment and stays there for a long time.

      • You can easily find sulfate-free products from several big brands

  • Moisturizers and makeup that contain BHA and BHT, which may disrupt your hormones.

    • They both have tendencies to bioaccumulate, are toxic to aquatic organisms, and cause genetic mutations in amphibians.

6. Engage in Earth Day Activities on April 22nd

Whether you want to engage in a beach clean up, bring your family together to encourage environmental awareness, or just contribute to any specific environmental issue, there’s an activity out there on Earth Day. It’s the perfect day to give back to our planet and reflect on your current environmental habits. Have you ever stopped and wondered about how many ‘earths’ would be required to sustain your lifestyle, if everyone on the planet lived the same life you did? Take the test. Is the number higher than you’d expect?

Check out our Habits page to develop environmentally-friendly habits and to learn how to sustain them throughout the following year.
