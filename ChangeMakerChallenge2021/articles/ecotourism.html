Ecotourism in South Florida

Nature’s beauty surrounds us every day, but if you ever need a reminder, try out ecotourism! According to the International Ecotourism Society, ecotourism is defined as “responsible travel to natural areas that conserves the environment, sustains the well-being of the local people, and involves interpretation and education” (TIES, 2015). With ecotourism, you are granted the chance to visit fascinating places, with many being in South Florida. As you witness the specialties of our ecosystems, take a look around you and appreciate our environment. When you are doubting the impacts of your actions to stop climate change, let these fun experiences encourage you to continue being considerate of our planet.

Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden

Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden is a “tropical wonderland,” as described by its own website. Fairchild is home to a beautiful selection of plants, including rare, tropical ones and native flowers. It also focuses on educating its visitors, as it offers school programs, scholarships, and even graduate fellowships.

Pelican Harbor Seabird Station

Since its founding in 1980, the Pelican Harbor Seabird Station has done priceless work, rescuing over 35,821 native birds, mammals, and reptiles. Visitors are given the opportunity to volunteer to care for the animals being taken care of by cleaning after and feeding them. The organization also provides “Seabird Cruises,” which take passengers along waters to observe over 30 species of birds.

Biscayne National Park

The Biscayne National Park is one of the prides of Florida that allows visitors to take a break from the busy streets of downtown Miami for relaxation in nature. The park works to conserve and protect a unique blend of aquamarine waters, emerald islands, and fish-bejeweled coral reefs. Visitors are able to boat, snorkel, camp, watch wildlife, or just take a walk along the stunning views.

Arthur R. Marshall Loxahatchee National Wildlife Refuge

The Arthur R. Marshall Loxahatchee National Wildlife Refuge is a wildlife sanctuary located in Palm Beach County, Florida. It houses the most northern remnant of the historic Everglades wetland ecosystem.

Gumbo Limbo

One of my personal favorites, Gumbo Limbo is a fun, interactive nature center focused on rehabilitating marine wildlife, specifically sea turtles. You can adopt a sea turtle, go on a walk on their Boardwalk Trail, check out their Butterfly Garden, and so much more. It is a great place to take children to learn about the importance of nature. Plus, they are often involved in beach cleanups!
