An Environmentally Friendly Florida Summer

When thinking about Florida, thoughts of summer sun, beaches, and fun immediately come to mind, and with good reason. Summertime is the peak of the year for Floridian residents as well as tourists. However, it is becoming increasingly likely that our beloved beaches will be lost to the growing problem that is global warming.


Wondering how to help? Read on for some suggestions of how to do your part for the environment during summer!

1. Beat the Heat

It's no secret that summer temperatures make us feel like we’re about to explode, and the natural response is to crank up the air conditioning.


Avoid doing this.


This is one of the many instincts that the global population needs to unlearn if we want to save our planet. Instead of cooling the air around you, cool your own body! A chilled glass of water does wonders for body temperature. So does a wet washcloth across the forehead or rubbing an ice cube over your wrists. Take advantage of the heat! Cold showers save energy and cool down the body. Additionally, it forces you to spend less time in the shower, therefore wasting less water.



2. Using environmentally friendly products

Using the wrong products contributes to the loss of beaches. The two most common ones? Deodorant and sunscreen.


When sticky sweat from the heat worsens a day, we apply deodorant. Our advice? Make sure it is environmentally friendly, unlike most of the big brands. A great example of an eco-friendly brand is Toms! That is mainly due to the fact that triclosan, a chemical that suppresses body odor, can be very harmful to animals when it gets into an aquatic, sunny environment. Even when we shower at night and the day’s deodorant washes down the drain, it enters waterways and sits atop the water’s surface, just waiting to be licked up by some unsuspecting animal. To make matters worse, the packaging is often non-recyclable and non-biodegradable. Since deodorant is so commonly put on people’s bodies, it’s important to make sure we are using eco-friendly, natural products for both ourselves and the environment.


Additionally, spray deodorant is a huge contributor to air pollution because it emits volatile organic compounds that contribute to ground-level ozone levels, a key component of smog. This breaks down the ozone layer, which causes the sun to have greater effects on humans. The solution? Sunscreen.

At this point, it most likely doesn’t come as a surprise that sunscreen hurts the environment as well. Between 6000 and 14,000 tons of sunscreen washes off of ocean swimmers per year, then finding its way to coral reefs and seriously harming them. Four major ingredients have been proven as threats to coral.

  • Oxybenzone: a chemical that absorbs harmful sun rays. More importantly, it deranges coral reproduction, damages its DNA, and bleaches it.

  • Butylparaben: a preservative that bleaches coral.

  • Octinoxate: filters out harmful sun rays and bleaches coral as well.

  • 4-methylbenzylidene camphor: filters out harmful sun rays and bleaches coral. Luckily, this ingredient is banned in the USA.


Some eco-friendly, ocean-friendly, and reef-safe sunscreen brands you can use include:

  • Badger SPF 35 Sport Sunscreen, which uses a reef-safe mineral Zinc Oxide and contains no oxybenzone, octinoxate, or other harsh chemical active ingredients. It is unscented, biodegradable, 100% certified natural, and 94% certified organic. The tube is also recyclable!

  • Life Basics SPF 30 All Natural Sunscreen is a natural, vegan sunblock. It contains active ingredients Zinc Oxide and other naturally-derived ingredients such as calendula, green tea, and reship oil which help to nourish the skin.

  • Wawai’s Organics Ph is an ocean-friendly, all-natural, Philippines-made sunscreen that is packaged in refillable canisters.

  • Thinksport SPF 50+ Sunscreen eco-friendly sunscreen contains 20% non nano Zinc Oxide, which is safer for the oceans, and has no biologically toxic chemicals.

  • WOTNOT Sunscreen SPF 30 is a skin-friendly and ocean-friendly sunscreen that is made of zinc oxide. It is also cruelty-free, gluten-free, and palm oil-free.


3. Cleaning Up Our Beaches

In addition to the sea level rising, we could also potentially lose our sandy vacation spot to the enormous heaps of garbage piling up on our shores. You can't exactly blame the sharks for that one. Every soda can you carelessly toss onto the beach after a nice lunch adds to this problem. Not only does it look and smell awful, but it also only takes one gust of wind to blow said soda can into the water and kill an animal.


If a dead shark washed up on the shore, would you really want to stay there? Absolutely not.

So do your part. Rid yourself of the mindset that littering one time won’t make a difference just because you responsibly get rid of your trash every other time. It only takes one piece of trash to take the life of an innocent animal. Take part in local beach cleanups. If your community has none available, organize one! It only takes one person to make a difference, but a few friends can always help.
