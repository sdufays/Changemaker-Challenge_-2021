Public Transportation in South Florida

It is no secret that transportation is the biggest contributor to air pollution. In fact, in 2018, the EPA found that 28% of greenhouse gas emissions were due to transportation. These emissions go on to cause air pollution, which results in a multitude of diseases, ranging from pneumonia to cancer. Thankfully, we can work to reduce this by increasing our use of public transportation, for the Kansas City Area Transportation Authority explains that a single person who switches from commuting alone by car to public transportation, can reduce their annual CO2 emissions by more than 48,000 pounds annually, the equivalent to a “10% reduction in all greenhouse gases produced by a typical two-adult, two-car household.” This is because public transportation produces far less air pollutants, such as carbon monoxide and carbon dioxide. Thus, one of the best ways we can help stop climate change is to move from riding cars to riding buses, subways, and any other types of public transportation.


Most Floridians believe that there is no public transport whatsoever in South Florida; However, this is not the case. Below are examples of public transportation specific to South Florida, primarily Miami.


https://www.theunderline.org/

The Underline, although still in its planning stages, is aiming to transform the insufficiently used land below Miami’s Metrorail into a 10-mile linear park, urban trail and public art destination. It is preparing to connect downtown Miami to its southern suburbs.


http://www.citibikemiami.com/

The Citi Bike program is Miami’s bike sharing and rental system. It gives users the option of up to 2,000 bikes, strategically placed throughout 160 locations near and in Miami. This program is also powered by solar energy.


https://www8.miamidade.gov/global/transportation/metrorail.page


The Miami Metrorail system is a 25-mile rail system that stretches from Miami International Airport to South Miami. It is a great option for those looking to cut their carbon footprint by engaging in public transportation.


Trolleys


The major cities of South Florida, including Miami, Miami Beach, and Coral Gables offer free trolley services to link tourists to popular destinations.

https://www.gobrightline.com/


At 100% carbon neutral, Brightline is without a doubt, far more beneficial for the environment than cars are. It is an innovative, high-speed train, with its main ambition being connecting Miami to Orlando.



Although we don’t have much available, taking public transportation whenever possible will greatly benefit the environment, and increasing demand for these eco-friendly options will also encourage more and more to emerge. Of course, while considering public transportation, please keep in mind COVID guidelines. Safety should always be your first priority.
