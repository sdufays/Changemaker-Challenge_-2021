Making Habits

At Clean Step, we believe that consistency is the only tool you can use to truly pave a path towards a better life.

First, how do we make a habit? Different techniques work for different people, but here are a few general ideas to help you find what works best for you:

  • Start simple

  • Place reminders

  • Be consistent

  • Convince friends/family to join you

  • Know the benefits and write them down

  • Be positive!

Don’t forget to use our habit tracker and habit formation sheet to guide you through this process! You’re never alone!

Solidifying Habits Through Self-Reflection

Although it’s less emphasized, reflecting on habits is just as important as doing them. You’ll learn more about yourself and more about how to become better, and this can help you stick to both your personal and ecological goals long-term.

So, here are some general questions to ask yourself about your habits.

  • Is there a particular habit that I’m not doing as often?
  • If so, why is that? Is it because I find it uncomfortable or too difficult? Why do I feel that way?
  • If I find a specific habit uncomfortable, how should I structure my cues and rewards for that habit so that I actually want to do it?
  • What do I consider to be my “worst” habit and why? What makes me want to do that habit? How can I combat that craving by replacing it with something else?

    • Visualization: What would my day look like if I did not have that habit?

  • What do I consider to be my “easiest” or most natural habit and why? What makes me want to do that habit? How can I structure my other habits so that I can make other habits feel natural?

    • Visualization: What would my day look like if I had more good habits that felt natural to me?

Now, for the beginning of your day, even if you’ve woken up on the wrong side of the bed, here are some questions to answer to make every day start off well.

  • How do I want to feel today?

    • Ex: Strong, capable, energetic, excited, loving, relaxed, grateful.

  • What do I want to be today?

    • Ex: Aware, intelligent, kind, compassionate, productive, eco-friendly.

  • What am I going to do to achieve those goals?

    • Ex: Exercise, learn something new, read a book, help a friend with X, recycle X items, go into nature, journal, compost.

Remember, regardless of whether you’ve checked off every habit on your list at 9:00AM or if you haven’t done a single thing you wanted to until 9:00PM, every step matters. As long as you start and continue trying, your efforts will pay off for both yourself and the planet. We’re rooting for you!
